Classical Management Theory

1. Scientific Management (Frederick Taylor)

The Classical Theory of Management emerged during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. It focused on improving productivity and organizational efficiency through scientific methods.

Frederick Winslow Taylor introduced Scientific Management, which emphasized time studies, standardization, and proper training of workers. Henri Fayol developed Administrative Management Theory, identifying key management functions such as planning, organizing, leading, and controlling.

Max Weber contributed the Bureaucratic Theory, which focused on rules, hierarchy, and formal structure within organizations. The classical approach aimed to create order, discipline, and efficiency in large organizations.
