Pre-scientific Management Period

Employees went to their work instead of receiving it, and so, the factory system, as it is known today, became a dominant feature of the economy. Under this system, land and buildings, hired labor, and capital were made available to the entrepreneur, who strives to combine these factors in the efficient achievement of a particular goal.

All these changes brought about developments in the field of management. Traditional, conventional, or customary ideas of management were slowly given up, and management came to be based on scientific principles.

In the words of L. F. Urwick, "Modern management has thrown open a new branch of human knowledge, a fresh universe of discourse".

During the period following the Industrial Revolution, certain pioneers challenged traditional management by introducing new ideas and approaches.
