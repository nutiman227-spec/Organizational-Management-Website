Pre-scientific Management Period

The Pre-scientific Management Period refers to the time before management was recognized as a formal field of study. During this period, there were no systematic management principles. Organizations were small, and managers relied mainly on personal experience, intuition, and trial-and-error methods.

With the Industrial Revolution, production shifted from homes to factories. Workers went to factories instead of working in their own homes. Because of this change, managers needed better ways to supervise employees, control production, and improve efficiency.

However, there were still no scientific methods being applied. Management practices were informal and depended mostly on tradition. This period became the foundation for the development of modern management theories.
